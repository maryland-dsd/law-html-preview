.01 Definitions.

A. In this chapter, the following terms have the meanings indicated.

B. Terms Defined.

(1) “Endangered species” means any species whose continued existence as a viable component of the State's fish resources is determined to be in jeopardy, including any species of fish determined to be an "endangered species" pursuant to the Endangered Species Act of 1973, 16 U.S.C. §§1531—1543.

(2) “Species in need of conservation” means any species which:

(a) May become threatened in the foreseeable future;

(b) Has a limited or declining population; and

(c) Has been determined by the Secretary to be in need of conservation measures for its continued ability to sustain itself successfully.

(3) “Threatened species” means any species of fish which appears likely, within the foreseeable future, to become endangered, including any species of fish determined to be a "threatened species" pursuant to the Endangered Species Act of 1973, 16 U.S.C. §§1531—1543.

.02 Factors for Endangered or Threatened Determination.

A. Determination Pursuant to the Endangered Species Act.

(1) Any species of fish determined to be an endangered species pursuant to the Endangered Species Act shall be considered an endangered species under the provisions of this subtitle.

(2) Any species of fish determined to be a threatened species pursuant to the Endangered Species Act shall be considered a threatened species under the provisions of this subtitle.

(3) The Secretary may determine, in accordance with this regulation, that any threatened species is an endangered species throughout all or any portion of the range of the species within the State.

B. Determination Pursuant to Other Factors. In addition to the species considered to be endangered or threatened pursuant to the Endangered Species Act, the Secretary, by rule or regulation, shall determine whether any species of fish normally occurring within the State is an endangered or threatened species due to any of the following factors:

(1) The present or threatened destruction, modification, or curtailment of its habitat or range;

(2) Overutilization for commercial, sporting, scientific, educational, or other purposes;

(3) Disease or predation;

(4) The inadequacy of existing regulatory mechanisms; or

(5) Other natural or manmade factors affecting its continued existence within the State.

.04 Prohibitions.

A. Except as authorized by Natural Resources Article, §4-2A-08(a), Annotated Code of Maryland, or by a permit issued by the Secretary under §B of this regulation, a person may not take, possess, transport, export, process, sell, offer for sale, deliver, carry, or ship any fish of the species listed in Regulation .03A and B of this chapter.

B. Permit to Take or Possess Endangered or Threatened Species.

(1) The Secretary may issue a permit, under the terms and conditions he prescribes, to allow the taking or possession of species listed in Regulation.03A and B of this chapter for:

(a) Scientific purposes;

(b) Enhancing the propagation and survival of a species; or

(c) Aquaculture purposes in accordance with COMAR 08.02.14.

(2) Violation of any provision or restriction of the permit shall constitute a violation of this regulation and may result, at the discretion of the Secretary, in revocation of the permit and confiscation of those species held pursuant to it.

C. A person may take, possess, transport, export, process, sell, or offer for sale, or ship those species designated as in need of conservation in Regulation .03E of this chapter unless prohibited by regulations adopted by the Department for each individual species.

D. Penalty. Violation of any provision of this regulation is subject to all penalties and actions included in Natural Resources Article, §§4-2A-07 and 4-1207, Annotated Code of Maryland. This may result in seizure of the fishing license and ultimate confiscation of all fish, fishing gear, equipment, and vessel used in the fishing activity.
