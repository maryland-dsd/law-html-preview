.01 Scope.

A. These regulations outline procedures for the payment of fees for certain hearings conducted by the Office.

B. These regulations do not apply to:

(1) State government offices, agencies, or other units of State government;

(2) County or municipal governments;

(3) Individual parties in hearings conducted under State Personnel and Pensions Article, Annotated Code of Maryland; or

(4) Individual parties in hearings regarding claims for assistance or reimbursement of assistance involving:

(a) Developmental disabilities pursuant to Health-General Article, Title 7, Annotated Code of Maryland;

(b) Mental health pursuant to Health-General Article, Title 10, Subtitle 7, Annotated Code of Maryland;

(c) Assistance programs pursuant to Health-General Article, Title 15, Annotated Code of Maryland;

(d) Special Supplemental Food Program for Women, Infants, and Children pursuant to Health-General Article, Â§18-108, Annotated Code of Maryland;

(e) Public assistance pursuant to Human Services Article, Title 5, Annotated Code of Maryland;

(f) Community services pursuant to Human Services Article, Title 6, Annotated Code of Maryland; or

(g) A request for order of eligibility or a petition for supplemental compensation pursuant to State Finance and Procurement Article, Â§10-501, Annotated Code of Maryland.

.03 Payment of Filing Fees.

A. Except as provided in Regulations .01B and .06 of this chapter, a request for a hearing shall be accompanied by payment of:

(1) A $150 filing fee for a hearing related to violation of the Maryland Vehicle Law; or

(2) A $50 filing fee for other types of hearings for which a filing fee is required.

B. Payment of the Filing Fee.

(1) Payment of the filing fee may be by:

(a) Certified check;

(b) Cashier's check;

(c) Money order; or

(d) Personal check.

(2) The Office may not accept cash as payment of a filing fee.

C. The Office shall schedule a hearing only upon receipt of a valid hearing request.

.04 Payment of Subpoena Processing Fees.

A. Except as provided in Regulations .01B and .06 of this chapter, requests for subpoenas shall be accompanied by payment of a $5 processing fee for each subpoena requested.

B. Payment of Subpoena Fee.

(1) Payment of the subpoena fee may be by:

(a) Certified check;

(b) Cashier's check;

(c) Money order; or

(d) Personal check.

(2) The Office may not accept cash as payment of a subpoena fee.

C. If a subpoena is not issued by the Office, the fee shall be returned.

.05 Assessment of Penalties for Insufficient Checks.

A. A personal check that is returned for insufficient funds shall be turned over to the Central Collection Unit of the Department of Budget and Fiscal Planning for collection.

B. An additional fee of $25 shall be assessed on all checks returned for insufficient funds.

.06 Waiver of Fees.

A. Eligibility Criteria. The Office shall waive filing and subpoena processing fees for a person that, when the request is filed:

(1) Is exempt from filing and subpoena processing fees under Regulation .01B of this chapter;

(2) Is represented by counsel retained through any legal services program which receives State funding or funding through the Maryland Legal Services Corporation; or

(3) Applies for and is granted a waiver based on indigence under Â§B of this regulation.

B. Requests for Fee Waivers Based on Indigence.

(1) Indigent persons may apply for a fee waiver by including with their request for a hearing or request for a subpoena:

(a) Documentation that they are receiving assistance listed in Regulation .01B(4) of this chapter; or

(b) An appropriate affidavit of indigence with supporting documentation.

(2) An Affidavit of Indigence form, and instructions for completing it, are available:

(a) On the Office of Administrative Hearings website; and

(b) By mail upon request.

(3) If a request for a fee waiver is received without an affidavit demonstrating indigence:

(a) Within 5 calendar days after receipt of the request, the Office shall mail the requesting person an Affidavit of Indigence form with instructions; and

(b) Within 14 calendar days after the form was mailed, the requesting person shall file the completed form and any supporting documentation.

(4) If it determines that the person is indigent, the Office shall grant the requested fee waiver or waivers.

C. The Office may modify or waive any time periods established by this chapter for good cause.

.07 Failure to Pay Required Fees.

A. Unless a waiver is granted under Regulation .06 of this chapter, failure to pay a required filing fee may result in the dismissal of a personâs request for a hearing.

B. Unless a waiver is granted under Regulation .06 of this chapter, failure to pay a required subpoena processing fee may result in a subpoena not being issued.

.08 Refunds of Fees.

A. Refunds.

(1) Except as provided in Â§A(2) of this regulation, the Office shall refund a filing or subpoena processing fee if the person that paid the fee receives a favorable decision.

(2) The Office shall not refund:

(a) Fees that were waived under Regulation .06 of this chapter; or

(b) Processing fees for subpoenas that were issued.

B. The Office shall process and mail a refund within 30 days after:

(1) The person that paid the fee receives a favorable decision;

(2) The presiding ALJ certifies the favorable decision; and

(3) The person that paid the fee provides a correct mailing address and other information required by the Office.
