.03 Personnel.

A. The Secretary shall designate one or more Department employee representatives to be responsible for the purchase and distribution of prescription drugs and devices for public health purposes.

B. A designated Department representative shall:

(1) Be a merit or contractual employee of the Department employed full time in a managerial level position;

(2) Be aware of and knowledgeable about the policies and procedures pertaining to the purchase and distribution of prescription drugs and devices for public health purposes, including applicable State and federal laws;

(3) Be actively involved in, and aware of, the daily operation of the Department’s purchasing and distribution of prescription drugs and prescription devices;

(4) Be responsible for:

(a) Storage and handling as set forth in Regulation .04 of this chapter;

(b) Record-keeping requirements as set forth in Regulation .06 of this chapter; and

(c) Making records available for inspection; and

(5) Meet the following qualifications:

(a) Is 21 years old or older;

(b) Except for an authorized absence such as sick leave or vacation leave, is physically present at the Department during regular business hours;

(c) Does not have any convictions for a violation of any federal, State, or local laws relating to wholesale or retail prescription drug distribution or distribution of controlled substances; and

(d) Does not have any convictions for a felony under federal, State, or local laws.
